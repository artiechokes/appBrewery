Religion and Faith

Each day, it's important to take your bearings. Where are you today? In this Lenten exercise, in the scriptures, in your daily life, and in your very self? Spend just a minute or two getting your bearings for the day.

Let's briefly examine where we are in this Lenten exercise and then begin each day with a prayer for transformation--for ourselves, our fraternity brothers, and all Exodus Men.

You are in the borderland between Gentile and Jew. As we continue to follow Christ, we find ourselves constantly going back and forth in our relationship with God. At times, we find ourselves in a familiar setting, relating to the God we know, resting in traditions and customs that comfort and console us. At other times we are confronted by something new and challenging: we begin to understand our faults and failings anew, and are challenged to grow in new ways. God wants us in this borderland. It is where Jesus kept his disciples as he prepared them to encounter his Paschal Mystery. And so we follow the disciples and Christ, at times journeying through the familiar areas of Judea, repeating the customs of Lent that we expect, and at other times, we set out into pagan lands to discover our need for renewed conversion. In this back and forth, we need to stay focused on Christ. Until now we have been thinking of the works we are doing to be transformed: prayer, fasting, and almsgiving. Now we want to turn our attention to the activities we can do that are a sharing in Christ’s inner life. Jesus wants us to share in his own knowledge of God, to be transformed through faith. As we enter into the unknown, let us renew and deepen our faith in Christ. Let us be transformed.

Pray the Lord blesses you and your fraternity with a foundation for real personal change during this season of Lent.

A Reading from the Gospel of Mark

Now when the Pharisees gathered together to him, with some of the scribes, who had come from Jerusalem, they saw that some of his disciples ate with hands defiled, that is, unwashed. For the Pharisees, and all the Jews, do not eat unless they wash their hands, observing the tradition of the elders; and when they come from the marketplace, they do not eat unless they purify themselves; and there are many other traditions which they observe, the washing of cups and pots and vessels of bronze. And the Pharisees and the scribes asked him, “Why do your disciples not live according to the tradition of the elders, but eat with hands defiled?” And he said to them, “Well did Isaiah prophesy of you hypocrites, as it is written, ‘This people honors me with their lips, but their heart is far from me; in vain do they worship me, teaching as doctrines the precepts of men.’ You leave the commandment of God, and hold fast the tradition of men.

Reflection

The story in today’s reading is almost comical: Jesus has just miraculously fed 5,000 men, with twelve baskets of food left over. And the only thing that the Pharisees can say is, you didn’t wash your hands before you ate. What incredible blindness! What a willful refusal to acknowledge what has happened right before their eyes!

Here we see an hypocrisy that is an obstacle to true faith. The Pharisees had received the commandments of God that they were to meditate upon day and night. The people of the Mosaic covenant accepted the law because it was revealed by God who is truth itself. But for this faith is to be living and active, the people needed to understand what had been revealed. The Pharisees were well-known experts in the law and held themselves to be more scrupulous in keeping the law.

But at some point in the history of the Jewish people, many of the Pharisees lost sight of God and his loving will and began to delight more in themselves and their own prestige and power to create legal traditions among the Jews. Washing of hands is a case in point: nowhere in the law does God specify that all Jews must ritually wash before eating. The Pharisees, however, had extended the priestly requirement to eat in ritual purity to all of Israel as a way of demonstrating the link between all eating and eating in connection with Temple worship.

Those Pharisees who had developed this aspect of the law were delighted by themselves and their own power. If their faith had been rooted in humble obedience to God, they would have been able to recognize Jesus as the Son of God and marvel at his teaching and miracles. But because their faith had become more about themselves than about God, they were unable to respond to the revelation of Christ.

As we submit to our disciplines and rules of life, we must always remember that those external actions are not a replacement for the love of God and obedience to the will of God. Pope Francis writes, “Believing means entrusting oneself to a merciful love... which sustains and directs our lives.... Faith consists in the willingness to let ourselves be constantly transformed and renewed by God’s call” (Lumen Fidei, #13). The personal, intimate relationship with God that he has given us is the essence of our faith. We must always keep in mind that it is God who sustains our life of faith and not merely our own thoughts and efforts.

Week 4 Action Items

1. Embrace silence. God brings about our transformation through prayer. We need to prioritize silent time with him each day. Missing prayer time can seem just like passing by a gas station exit on the highway. It's passed before we even realize it. If you want to become a holier man, put on the brakes to make time for the source of all grace and life: Jesus Christ.
The disciplines don’t mean much if our hearts and minds aren’t being led in the right direction in silent prayer. Let the Word of God lead you through this desert each day.

2. Be diligent. Stay the course by trading desserts for spiritual diligence. Keep up all your efforts to the end. Christ has great things in mind for you.
As you continue to maintain discipline through penance and exercise, be sure not to waste the opportunity to offer these difficulties for your past sins. Embrace this opportunity, this time of renewal.

3. Stay faithful to your brothers. They need you and you need them regardless of whether or not everyone in your fraternity believes this. Show up to your weekly fraternity meeting, be truthful, and let God do the rest.
